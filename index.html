Our Approach

Mathematics as a Way of Thinking

We support when needed, challenge when appropriate, inculcate sound reasoning skills, and encourage articulate communication of mathematical ideas.

The Mathematics Department aspires to lead all of our students to greater understanding of and appreciation for the power of mathematics. From algebra and geometry through calculus, statistics, and beyond, students learn to approach problems by integrating mathematical modeling, creative thought, and previous experience.

We aim to impart a genuine sense of the utility, power, and beauty of mathematics. As students progress through our curriculum, we hope that they will grow into the mathematics they explore — developing the confidence to understand theorems, to use techniques appropriately, to test results for reasonability, and to welcome new challenges whose outcomes are unknown. Students will graduate from Middlesex empowered by their own hard-won understanding — equipped not just with skills, but with the habits of mind that make those skills meaningful.

For Incoming Students

Your Math Journey Begins with Placement

All Middlesex students arrive having studied some algebra. The placement test helps us understand where you are — not judge where you should be. Students may be placed at any level depending on their background. The placement test has two parts, and both are administered with MyOpenMath.

1

Part 1: Algebra Foundations

This is to be taken by ALL incoming students. It is designed to take approximately 1–1.5 hours, but it is not strictly timed and you may complete it in multiple sittings.

2

Part 2: Advanced Algebra & Trigonometry

The second part covers advanced algebra and trigonometry topics and should be taken by students who have studied more than one full year of algebra, or those looking for more of a challenge after completing Part 1.

You may use a calculator throughout the Placement Test, but you should use no other outside aids.

The Department will review placement test results and placements will be communicated to you along with other course information later in the summer.

Where might I be placed?

Many new students are placed in foundational or intermediate courses, but some arrive ready for higher-level work. There is no single "right" entry point — advancement is based on mastery, not pace. You complete whichever parts of the test are appropriate, and the department places you based on the full picture.

Before you start the test...

Have these ready:

  • A calculator
  • Paper to work on
  • Something to write with

Please work independently — do not consult textbooks, old notebooks, or any online or human resources.
